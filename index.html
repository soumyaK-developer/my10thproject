Chicken curry

To make chicken curry, begin by heating oil in a pan and sautéing diced onions until golden. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder. Stir well. Next, add chicken pieces and cook until browned. Pour in coconut milk or tomato puree, along with salt and any desired vegetables. Cover and simmer until chicken is cooked through and flavors meld together. Adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot with rice or naan. Optionally, you can add yogurt or cream for a creamier curry. Enjoy the aromatic and flavorful chicken curry!

Saahi paneer

To make saahi paneer, start by heating oil in a pan and adding cumin seeds, chopped onions, ginger-garlic paste, and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown. Then, add tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook the masala until the oil separates. Next, add fresh cream and simmer for a minute. Finally, add cubed paneer (Indian cottage cheese) and cook until it absorbs the flavors. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice. Saahi paneer is a delicious, creamy dish that can be enjoyed by vegetarians and paneer lovers alike.

Raaj kachori

To make Raj Kachori, start by mixing 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup of semolina, and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough. Let it rest for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, prepare the filling by mixing boiled and mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, sev (crunchy chickpea flour noodles), and tamarind chutney. Take small portions of the dough, roll them into flat discs, and deep fry until golden brown and crispy. Once cooled, make a hollow in each disc and fill them with the prepared filling. Top with yogurt, mint chutney, and garnish with chopped coriander, sev, and pomegranate seeds. Serve immediately. Enjoy the flavorful Raj Kachori!

Pav bhaji

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish consisting of a spicy vegetable curry served with buttery bread rolls. To make pav bhaji, start by boiling and mashing a mix of vegetables like potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrots. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté onions until golden brown, then add ginger-garlic paste and chopped tomatoes. Once the tomatoes are cooked, add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Mix in the mashed vegetables and simmer until flavors meld. Finally, toast the pav (bread rolls) with butter, serve alongside the bhaji, and garnish with chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime. Enjoy!

Rajma chawal

To make Rajma Chawal, start by soaking 1 cup of kidney beans (rajma) overnight. Drain the water and cook the rajma in a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water, salt, and a pinch of turmeric until tender. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and sauté 1 finely chopped onion until golden brown. Add 2 teaspoons of ginger-garlic paste, 2 chopped tomatoes, and cook until the tomatoes soften. Add 1 tablespoon of Rajma masala, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Mix well, then add the cooked rajma along with its liquid. Simmer for 10 minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice.
